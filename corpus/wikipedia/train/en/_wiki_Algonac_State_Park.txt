
Algonac State Park is a public recreation area covering 1,450 acres  along the St. Clair River, two miles north of the city of Algonac in St. Clair County, Michigan. The state park's half mile of river frontage offers a view of passing international freighters. 4 
The park was established in 1937 with initial state acquisition of 373 acres of land. Over the next ten years, additional acquisitions increased the park size to approximately 980 acres. Park development began in the 1940s. 5 
Approximately 83% of the park ) is undeveloped. The undeveloped portion encompasses significant "lakeplain" prairie and savannas , described as unique natural environments in Michigan. 6  The park is home to 22 species of plants, birds and butterflies classified by the state as endangered, threatened, or of special concern. 4 
The park offers shoreline walleye fishing, hiking, camping, cross-country skiing, shooting range, picnicking facilities, and a playground. It is excellent for watching freighters and hunting. 4
