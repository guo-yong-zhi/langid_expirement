
Parajotus is a genus of African jumping spiders that was first described by George and Elizabeth Peckham in 1903. 2  As of August 2019 update  it contains only three species, found only in Africa: P. cinereus, P. obscurofemoratus, and P. refulgens. 1  The name is a combination of the Ancient Greek "para" , meaning "alongside", and the related genus Jotus.
